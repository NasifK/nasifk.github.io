Line Following Car Project, Introduction to Electrical Engineering

June 2021

During my introduction to engineering class, we had a line-following car competition using a standardized track. My car achieved 3rd place in a class of over 100 students for the fastest time to complete the track. A video of the car working is shown below in the projects section.

Micromouse Project, IEEE at UCLA

October 2020 - March 2021

During my remote freshman year, I built a robotics maze-solving mouse with a partner. To compete in the American Micromouse Competition. All programming was done on the STM32Cube IDE in C. Our mouse won a judges choice award for igenuity in designing and implementing a custom chassis to shrink the mouse down to micro-size.

During this class, I learned embeddedML on the STM-ARM based Sensortile board. I created a wearable shoulder motion detection system to aid in post-surgical recovery with a built-in alarm system. A video of the device working is shown below.
